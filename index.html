Employee Onboarding Workflow Redesign

Transforming a 10-day sequential process into a 5-day parallel workflow.

Project Overview

This analysis visualizes the transformation of the employee onboarding process. The original "As-Is" workflow was characterized by sequential handoffs between HR, IT, and managers, leading to significant delays. The new "To-Be" workflow introduces automation and parallelism to streamline communication and drastically reduce the time required to get a new employee ready. Use the tabs below to explore and compare the two distinct processes.

Current State: The Sequential Bottleneck

Each department waits for the previous one to act. This linear progression is inefficient, with manual handoffs creating significant delays and idle time between critical tasks.

Future State: The Parallel Advantage

An automated trigger initiates all onboarding tracks simultaneously. HR, IT, and the Manager can work in parallel, eliminating wait times and cutting the total onboarding time in half.

HR

Start Onboarding
Triggers All Processes
Create Employee File

IT

Auto-request for Equipment
Order & Configure Equipment

Manager

Auto-notification of New Hire
Create Training Plan & Assign Mentor
All actions complete: Welcome New Employee!

The Result: 50% Time Reduction

By shifting from a sequential to a parallel model, the onboarding process becomes dramatically more efficient, reliable, and faster for new hires.
